Enhan Kang

I'm currently a third-year undergraduate student in Intelligence Engineering at Beihang University, enrolled in the Top Talents Program in Future Aerospace Technology. I'm fortunate to be advised by Prof. Zhiwei Zhang and Prof. Jinhu Lv.

My research interests focus on embodied intelligence, robotics, and swarm intelligence, with the broader goal of building intelligent systems that can perceive, coordinate, and act in complex real-world environments. I am particularly interested in integrating control theory, multi-agent systems, and generative models to enable scalable and adaptive autonomous agents.

Recently, my research has explored UAV swarm coordination and virtual-tube-based control strategies for cooperative flight in dynamic environments, aiming to improve collective robustness, adaptability, and task efficiency of aerial robot systems.

Outside of research, I enjoy various sports including badminton, table tennis, basketball, volleyball, and swimming. I am also interested in boxing, calligraphy, and playing the piano.
